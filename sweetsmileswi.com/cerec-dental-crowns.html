What is a CEREC Dental Crown?

CEREC stands for Chairside Economical Restoration of Esthetic Ceramics, which is an official way of saying that we use advanced technology to digitally create your crown. This procedure eliminates the need for unreliable and messy putty that has been used in the past for dental crowns. Plus, a digitally mapped version of your tooth leads to more precise results. After your restoration is digitally designed, it’s sent to our in-office milling machine where we will create your crown out of a single block of high-quality dental porcelain!

The CEREC Dental Crown Procedure

After we have decided that CEREC crowns are the right treatment for you, we’ll schedule your appointment. You’ll come into our dental office where we will ensure that you’re relaxed and comfortable before beginning. We will take digital impressions of the affected area. The CEREC technology combines a variety of images to create a 3D version of the crown for your tooth. This image is sent to our in-office milling machine, which crafts your crown. Next, we will make sure that you’re happy with the results before permanently bonding the crown to your affected tooth. All that’s left to do is to walk out of our dental office with a strong, beautiful, and functional smile.

Benefits of CEREC Dental Crowns

We offer CEREC crowns because of their many unique advantages, including:

  • Convenience: Patients can receive an amazing, custom-made restoration in just one appointment.
  • Personalization: We can customize the shape, shade, and size of your crown so it seamlessly blends into the rest of your smile.
  • Longevity: Our dental crowns are built to last for years to come with proper care. Additionally, dental porcelain is relatively stain-proof, making it difficult to discolor your restoration.
  • Affordability: In some cases, CEREC crowns are more affordable than traditional ones because they eliminate the need for multiple dental appointments.

If you’re ready to get started on an efficient and effective way to restore your smile, don’t hesitate to contact our dental office today!

If you need to repair damaged or decayed teeth, you may be researching CEREC dental crowns in Grafton. These restorations have become incredibly popular, and it’s easy to see why. Of course, we understand you probably have several questions in mind before moving forward with the process. How Long Will CEREC Dental Crowns Last?

Most patients can expect their CEREC dental crown to last anywhere between 10 and 15 years, which is about the same amount of time that a traditional crown lasts. The porcelain ceramic used to create these modern restorations are extremely durable and natural-looking. With proper care and maintenance, you can easily expect your crowned tooth to last well over a decade. You can make CEREC dental crowns last as long as possible by avoiding grinding your teeth or clenching them together, brushing and flossing daily, and visiting your dentist every six months.

Are CEREC Dental Crowns Worth It?

Absolutely! CEREC dental crowns in Grafton are far more efficient and technologically advanced than traditional crowns. Our practice can create your restorations in just a single appointment. Plus, they allow for a better bite and fit perfectly thanks to computer-aided technology. The entire process should only take about two hours, which is incredibly fast. You don’t have to worry about wearing a temporary crown or having uncomfortable impressions taken!

Is CEREC Safe?

Yes! CEREC Dental Crowns are as safe as traditional crowns. When we create your restorations, the process happens in another room. Our team will quickly fabricate your crown using our in-office milling machine. We’ll make sure you’re happy with the results before we permanently bond the restoration to the affected tooth. After it’s placed, you’ll be ready to show off your strong and healthy smile that very same day!

Does Getting a CEREC Dental Crown Hurt?

Not at all! It should feel about the same as any other dental procedure, maybe even with less discomfort. Our team will numb your mouth beforehand to ensure you don’t experience any pain. We may recommend over-the-counter pain medication to help ease any discomfort you may experience. If needed, you can ask about sedation dentistry to keep you relaxed throughout your appointment.
